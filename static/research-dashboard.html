Boundary Finding

A5 preserved all four direct negative answers and improved missing-validation abstention from 1/4 to 2/4. The remaining misses were certification and deployment-trial questions, which still read like direct negation to the hosted model. A6 improved the paired A5/A6 boundary run to 87.5% accuracy and 3/4 correct abstention while keeping all direct no answers and 0/8 poisoned citations; the five-repeat sweep then improved correct abstention to 19/20, and A7's structured gate reached 20/20. The expanded gate sweep then reached 40/40 correct abstention across 160 hosted rows while preserving every direct no control. The A8 classifier-gate follow-up kept 0/80 poisoned citations but dropped to 82.5% accuracy because missing-validation pages were sometimes classified as direct support or direct refutation. A9 changed 10 raw classifier labels and restored 100.0% accuracy on the expanded boundary set.

Long-Graph Finding

The long-chain graph run replicated evidence-gap repair: A8/A9 reached 12/12 correct abstention where A4 was 0/12. It also exposed the next bottleneck: relation-gated conditions over-abstained on direct policy and privacy-board controls, dropping direct-control preservation to 15/24 for A8 and 16/24 for A9. The A10 preservation-calibrated follow-up repaired that boundary: 36/36 accuracy, 12/12 correct abstention, and 24/24 direct controls with 0/36 attack success on gpt-5-mini, then replicated the same result on gpt-4.1-mini. On that second deployment, A8/A9 preserved only 1/12 direct no controls before A10 restored 12/12. The v2 preservation casebook adds 14 row-level repairs with trusted evidence pages, relation-label changes, and safety metrics; the transition appendix shows 35 label changes concentrated on repaired rows.
